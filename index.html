Ukraine hits oil and military facilities near Russia's St Petersburg

Al Jazeera (AP, Reuters)

A wave of Ukrainian long-range drones struck the St Petersburg region overnight July 4, hitting an oil terminal and a Baltic Sea port roughly 900km from Ukrainian-held territory — one of the largest deep-strike operations targeting Putin's home city. Russian authorities briefly halted flights at Pulkovo and throttled mobile internet to jam drone navigation. Ukraine's General Staff says its strikes have now disabled 42.74% of Russia's oil refining capacity since August 2025 (eight refineries hit in the past month, 60+ storage tanks damaged), with cumulative industry losses put at $13.5bn — independent analysts estimate closer to one-third capacity disrupted. Russia struck back at a Naftogaz gas facility in Poltava the same day.

Source: Al Jazeera (AP, Reuters)
